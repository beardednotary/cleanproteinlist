üí∞ The Cost of Safety Testing

Dr. Pieter Cohen (Harvard Medical School) explained to Consumer Reports why not all companies test rigorously:

"It can be more expensive to ensure that every vat of your product is very low in lead. And without a requirement that it be that way, it's unlikely that the industry as a whole is going to move in that direction."

The problem: FDA doesn't require pre-market testing. Companies that cut corners save money. Companies that test every lot spend more but produce safer products.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why Most Brands Don't Test As Much

Halpern suspects many companies rely on spot-checks or certificates of analysis from ingredient suppliers instead of testing every finished lot. This is cheaper and still technically meets FDA requirements - but allows contaminated batches to slip through.

FDA Still Has No Lead Limits for Protein Powders

Despite these encouraging results, there are still no federal limits for heavy metals in protein powders. The FDA doesn't require pre-market testing, and companies are left to self-regulate.

Consumer Reports' petition calling for strict FDA standards has garnered over 43,000 signatures since October 2025.
