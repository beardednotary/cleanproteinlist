What These Numbers Mean for Your Health

‚ö†Ô∏è The DHT Problem in Generic Creatine

Dihydrotriazine (DHT) at 110 mg/kg:

  • 35 times over the EFSA recommended safe limit (3 mg/kg)
  • Suspected carcinogen based on structural similarity to known carcinogens
  • If taking 5g daily of contaminated creatine = 0.55 mg DHT ingested daily
  • Long-term cumulative exposure is concerning

DCD (Dicyandiamide) contamination:

  • Converts to hydrogen cyanide in acidic stomach environment
  • Indicates poor manufacturing (incomplete synthesis)
  • Presence of DCD usually means DHT is also present
  • No safe level established because it shouldn't be there at all
