Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why doesn't Abbott publish heavy metal testing for Ensure?

A: Supplement and medical food manufacturers are not required to publicly disclose heavy metal testing results. While Abbott likely conducts internal testing for quality control, they don't share specific lead/cadmium/arsenic measurements. This is standard industry practice, but it means consumers have no way to verify safety independently.

Q: Is Ensure safer than protein powders because it's a medical food?

A: Not necessarily. "Medical food" is an FDA classification based on intended use (managing specific medical conditions), not a safety certification for heavy metals. Medical foods follow FDA regulations for manufacturing quality, but this doesn't guarantee low heavy metal content. The dairy-based protein in Ensure is *generally* less risky than plant proteins, but without testing we can't confirm it's safer than tested protein powders.

Q: Can I ask Abbott for heavy metal testing results?

A: You can contact Abbott customer service and request this information. However, they are not legally required to provide it and typically will not share specific heavy metal measurements. If enough consumers request this data, it may encourage Abbott to publish transparency reports.

Q: Should I avoid chocolate Ensure flavors?

A: Yes, we recommend avoiding chocolate-flavored Ensure products. Cocoa powder naturally accumulates lead and cadmium from soil. Consumer Reports and Clean Label Project both found significantly higher heavy metal levels in chocolate-flavored proteins (110x more cadmium in some cases). Stick with vanilla or unflavored Ensure if you must use it.

Q: Is OWYN Pro Elite a true replacement for Ensure?

A: OWYN Pro Elite provides higher protein (32g vs 9-30g depending on Ensure formula) with verified safety (lead below detection). However, Ensure formulas are complete nutrition shakes with added vitamins/minerals designed for specific medical conditions. OWYN is primarily a protein shake. For complete nutrition replacement, consult your doctor about whether OWYN meets your specific medical needs, or consider making custom shakes with verified-safe protein plus whole foods.

Q: Does Medicare/insurance cover OWYN like they cover Ensure?

A: Insurance coverage varies. Some Medicare Advantage plans and private insurance cover Ensure under durable medical equipment (DME) or nutritional support benefits. OWYN is typically not covered because it's classified as a dietary supplement, not a medical food. Check with your insurance provider. If cost is prohibitive, the DIY shake approach using verified-safe protein powder is more affordable ($2/serving vs $3.50 for OWYN).
