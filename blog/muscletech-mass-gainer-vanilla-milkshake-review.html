MuscleTech 100% Mass Gainer Vanilla Milkshake Review (2025)

Flavor Review: What Does “Vanilla Milkshake” Actually Taste Like?

If you’re picturing plain vanilla whey, this is not that. The Vanilla Milkshake flavor is designed to taste like a fast-food milkshake more than a subtle vanilla protein.

Sweetness & Overall Flavor

  • Sweetness: Medium-high. Most people with a sweet tooth will like it; if you hate sweet shakes, this may be too much.
  • Flavor profile: Vanilla ice-cream / melted milkshake. Works well with milk and oat milk, still decent with water.
  • Aftertaste: Very little “whey” taste if it’s fully blended; the carb blend and flavors cover it well.

Texture & Mixability

Remember: a full serving is 6 scoops (~357 g of powder). If you just dump that in a shaker bottle with a tiny bit of water, you’re going to hate life. With enough liquid and a blender, it’s actually very drinkable.

  • With a blender: Smooth, thick, and milkshake-like. This is the way to do a full serving.
  • In a shaker: Fine for ½ serving (3 scoops); a full 6-scoop serving will be thick and gritty.
  • Best combo: 16–24 oz of whole milk + ice + 3–4 scoops → legit dessert shake.
Pro tip: If you’ve ever hated mass gainers because they were chalky or “mud-thick,” try splitting the serving: 3 scoops after training and 3 scoops between meals. Same calories, much easier to drink.

Macros & Ingredients: What You’re Actually Drinking

Exact numbers vary a bit by region and retailer, but a typical full serving of Vanilla Milkshake looks roughly like this:

  • Calories: ~1,380–1,400
  • Protein: ~80 g (whey protein concentrate + whey protein isolate)
  • Carbohydrates: ~260–270 g (maltodextrin and other carb sources)
  • Fat: ~18 g
  • Sugar: ~10–20 g depending on batch and label

This is a true mass gainer, not a normal post-workout whey:

  • The macro split is roughly 23% protein / 60% carbs / 11% fat.
  • It’s built to push you into a calorie surplus quickly, not to be a “lean” protein shake.
  • For many hardgainers, one MuscleTech shake is basically an extra full meal.

Who the Vanilla Milkshake Flavor Is Perfect For

1. Classic Hardgainers

  • You struggle to gain weight even when you “eat a lot.”
  • You need 3,500–5,000+ calories per day just to move the scale.
  • You don’t want every meal to feel like a competitive eating contest.

One full shake can add roughly 1,400 calories on top of your normal food with very little effort. If you like vanilla desserts, this flavor makes that much easier.

2. Bulk Phases & Off-Season Athletes

  • Bodybuilders in a dedicated bulk.
  • Football / rugby / contact-sport athletes trying to move up a weight class.
  • College lifters who have the training volume to justify huge calorie intakes.

In these cases, the extra sweetness of Vanilla Milkshake is a feature, not a bug – you’re trying to get calories in quickly.

3. People Switching From Contaminated Mass Gainers

Lots of readers find this flavor because they’re bailing out of Naked Nutrition, ON Serious Mass, or other gainers after seeing the heavy-metal numbers from Consumer Reports.

  • You want a mass gainer, not a regular whey.
  • You care about contamination and long-term health.
  • You want a “safe but still tastes like a treat” option.

For that person, Vanilla Milkshake is often the easiest landing spot.

Who Should Not Use This Flavor

  • Anyone cutting or trying to lose weight. 1,400 calories is the opposite of what you want.
  • If you only need lean protein. You’re better off with something like BSN Syntha-6 or Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard.
  • If you’re vegan or strictly dairy-free. This is a whey-based product; OWYN Pro Elite is the only plant protein we recommend from a safety perspective (but it’s not a mass gainer).
  • If very sweet shakes bother you. In that case, start with ½ serving and extra water or milk to thin it out.

How to Mix Vanilla Milkshake So It Actually Tastes Like One

Full-Serving “Blender Bomb” (Bulking Mode)

  • 16–24 oz whole milk
  • 6 scoops MuscleTech 100% Mass Gainer Vanilla Milkshake
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: 1 banana or 1–2 tbsp peanut butter if you truly hate being lean

Blend 30–60 seconds. This is a 1,400–2,000+ calorie shake, depending on add-ins.

Half-Serving “Daily Driver” (Easier to Drink)

  • 12–16 oz milk or water
  • 3 scoops powder

Much easier on digestion, still adds ~700 calories and ~40 g protein. Great between meals or post-workout.
