Does Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Have Lead?

No. Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard 100% Whey Protein has lead below laboratory detection limits. That means the testing equipment used by Consumer Reports — some of the most sensitive analytical chemistry available — could not measure any lead in the product at all.

To put that in context: California's Prop 65 safe harbor limit for lead is 0.5 micrograms per day. A product at that threshold is considered acceptable. Optimum Nutrition tests at effectively zero — not 0.4, not 0.1, but below the floor of what the instrument can detect.

This result was confirmed across multiple production lots. Consumer Reports tests 2–4 distinct batches of each product to make sure a single clean batch isn't masking a contamination problem. ON Gold Standard held up across all lots tested.

✅ What "Below Detection" Means in Plain Terms

When a lab reports "below detection limits," it doesn't mean "a small amount." It means the instrument has a threshold — typically around 0.1 µg per serving for this type of testing — and the sample didn't register above it. In practical terms, Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Whey is as clean as it gets on lead contamination.
