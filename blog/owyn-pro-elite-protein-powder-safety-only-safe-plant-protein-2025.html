Why OWYN Is the Only Safe Plant Protein

The Plant Protein Contamination Problem

Why Plants Absorb Lead

Plants cannot distinguish between beneficial minerals and toxic heavy metals.

When pea, rice, and hemp plants grow in soil containing trace lead (which most agricultural soil does), they absorb it through their roots along with nutrients.

The lead gets concentrated in the plant protein - the exact part used to make protein powder.

Organic Makes It Worse

Organic farming practices increase heavy metal absorption.

Clean Label Project found organic plant proteins had 3x MORE lead than conventional plant proteins.

Why? Organic farming encourages soil-plant interaction (good for nutrient uptake, bad for metal uptake).

Most Brands Don't Test

The protein powder industry relies on "supplier certificates" rather than independent testing.

Suppliers say "our pea protein is clean" without verification. Brands accept this at face value and skip independent heavy metal testing of finished products.

Result: Contaminated products reach consumers without anyone knowing.
