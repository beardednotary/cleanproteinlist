Why 14 of 15 Plant Proteins Failed Safety Testing (And Why OWYN Didn't)

The fundamental problem: Plants are incredibly efficient at absorbing minerals from soil - including toxic heavy metals like lead, cadmium, and arsenic. When you concentrate plant material into protein powder, you concentrate the contamination.

How Plant Proteins Get Contaminated:

Step 1: Plants Absorb Heavy Metals from Soil

Peas, rice, hemp, and other protein source plants have extensive root systems that absorb minerals indiscriminately. If the soil contains lead (which most agricultural soil does from decades of leaded gasoline, industrial pollution), the plants absorb it.

Key insight: Organic farming doesn't solve this. Organic certification tests for pesticides, NOT heavy metals. Organic soil can still be contaminated with lead from historical pollution.

Step 2: Protein Extraction Concentrates Toxins

To make protein powder, manufacturers process tons of plant material to extract the protein. This concentration process magnifies whatever heavy metals were in the original plants.

Example: If 1,000 lbs of peas contains distributed lead, concentrating it into 100 lbs of pea protein powder means 10x concentration of lead per pound.

Step 3: Multiple Protein Sources = Multiple Contamination Vectors

Many plant proteins blend multiple sources (pea + rice + hemp). Each source brings its own contamination profile, compounding the problem.

Result: A "complete protein" blend often means complete contamination from multiple sources.

Vegan Protein Safety FAQ

Q: Why did 14 of 15 plant proteins fail if plants are supposed to be healthy?

A: Plants ARE healthy as whole foods. The problem is PROTEIN POWDER. When you concentrate plant material into powder, you concentrate whatever heavy metals were in the soil. Peas, rice, and hemp are particularly efficient at absorbing lead from contaminated soil. Consumer Reports found that plant proteins had 3-15x more lead than whey proteins on average.

Q: Can I just eat whole plant foods instead of using protein powder?

A: YES - this is actually the safest approach for vegans. Whole foods like lentils, chickpeas, quinoa, and tofu have much lower heavy metal concentrations than protein powder because you're not concentrating them. If you can meet your protein needs through whole foods (70-100g/day for most people), that's ideal. Use OWYN only when convenience requires it (post-workout, travel, busy mornings).

Q: I've been using Vega/Orgain/Garden of Life for years. Am I poisoned?

A: Not necessarily "poisoned," but you've likely accumulated more lead than ideal. Lead bioaccumulates slowly - it doesn't cause acute poisoning like food poisoning. Effects are cumulative and long-term (reduced cognitive function, fatigue, mood issues). If you've used contaminated plant protein daily for 1+ years, consider requesting blood lead testing from your doctor. Then switch to OWYN or whole food protein sources going forward.

Q: Why is OWYN safe when other plant proteins aren't? What do they do differently?
