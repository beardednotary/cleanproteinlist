Why Plant Proteins Have More Lead

üå± The Soil Absorption Problem

Plants absorb heavy metals from soil through their root systems. This is basic plant biology:

  • Peas, rice, and hemp are efficient at mineral uptake from soil
  • Lead in soil (from decades of leaded gasoline, industrial pollution, pesticides) gets absorbed alongside beneficial minerals
  • Protein concentration amplifies contamination: 20-30kg of peas ‚Üí 1kg protein powder = 20-30x concentration of any soil contaminants

Organic certification doesn't prevent this. Orgain is USDA Organic but still ranked #12 with 143% over safe lead limit. Organic soil can still contain lead from historical contamination.

The "Organic" Myth: Why Orgain Still Has Lead

Orgain is USDA Organic certified but still ranked #12 with 143% over safe lead limit. Here's why organic doesn't prevent heavy metal contamination:

What Organic Certification Actually Means

USDA Organic verifies:

  • ‚úÖ No synthetic pesticides or fertilizers
  • ‚úÖ No GMO ingredients
  • ‚úÖ No sewage sludge fertilizer
  • ‚úÖ Certified organic farming practices

USDA Organic does NOT test for:

  • ‚ùå Heavy metals (lead, arsenic, cadmium, mercury)
  • ‚ùå Historical soil contamination
  • ‚ùå Airborne pollutant deposition
  • ‚ùå Water source contamination

üö® The Historical Contamination Problem

Organic soil can still contain lead from decades past:

  • Leaded gasoline (banned 1996) deposited lead in soil across North America
  • Industrial pollution from nearby factories, smelters, mines
  • Historical pesticides (lead arsenate used until 1980s) remain in soil for 100+ years
  • Airborne deposition from coal plants, incinerators continues today

Plants don't discriminate. If lead is in the soil, organic crops absorb it just like conventional crops.
